## Rationale

Treatment B is a valid execution result, but it is not close to the H1/H2 capability floor and it does not show a new gain over Treatment A. That means the ablation is not answered yet.

The evidence supports a flat response at the Treatment B anchor-concentration level, not a regression and not a recovery. The remaining Treatment C arm is still needed to determine whether the sweep eventually turns upward again or stays flat.

## Why Not Stop Yet

- Treatment B does not reach or approach the H1 capability floor.
- Treatment B does not invalidate the controlled ablation design.
- Treatment B does not show an operational failure.
- The highest-concentration arm remains untested, so the causal curve is incomplete.

## Why Not Declare the Ablation Answered

Stopping now would leave the top end of the concentration sweep unresolved. Treatment B only tells us that the Control-to-A gain does not continue linearly into B.

That is useful, but not enough to close the design question.
