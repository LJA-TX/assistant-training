# Phase ZE Completion Report

## Executive Summary

Phase ZE is a documentation-only design phase.

The resulting experiment design isolates patch-local replacement topology as the next causal variable after the completed anchor-concentration sweep.

The design keeps fixed:

- the frozen scaffold,
- the exact-tool-request cue,
- the canonical `tool_calls` envelope,
- the anchor-heavy positive composition,
- the tool-positive density,
- the safety rows,
- and the validation surfaces.

Only patch locality, replacement distribution, and intervention footprint vary.

## Key Findings

1. H1 and H2 used a 100-row patch on a frozen scaffold.
2. Their replacement footprints were compact relative to the full corpus.
3. Phase ZD ruled out schema-only repair and weakened anchor concentration as a sufficient explanation.
4. Patch-local topology remains the untested variable with the highest information value.

## Recommended Design

Use a four-arm sweep:

- compact local control,
- two-cluster treatment,
- four-cluster treatment,
- fully dispersed treatment.

## Why This Is The Right Next Step

The experiment is smaller and cleaner than a new broad corpus redesign because it changes only one causal candidate:

- the geometry of the positive patch.

That gives the highest chance of learning whether H1/H2 depended on topology itself.
