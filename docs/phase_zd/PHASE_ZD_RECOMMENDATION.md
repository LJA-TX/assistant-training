- the frozen scaffold,

## Why This Is The Highest-Information Next Step

The completed anchor sweep already answered the anchor question:

- anchor concentration is contributory,
- but not sufficient.

The remaining unknown is whether the H1/H2 advantage depends on the **small, patch-local intervention shape** itself.

That is higher information than another broad redesign because:

1. Broad redesign already failed in Phase L, Phase Q, and Phase U.
2. The anchor sweep already showed that merely increasing anchor mass does not close the gap.
3. The next unresolved variable is the one H1/H2 still uniquely possessed: a small positive patch on a frozen scaffold.

## Why Not Another Full-Corpus Redesign

A broad redesign would reintroduce multiple confounders at once:

- tool-family balance,
- prompt-style breadth,
- safety calibration,
- and anchor density.

That would blur the causal signal that the completed ablation just sharpened.
